Events

Let's Rock !!!!!!

The students who engage in Cre8 can participate in a total of 19 events. They are Encore, FusionX, Project Runway,The Crown, Future Project, Make a Beat, Gaming, Painting, Photography, Story Wall, Short Film, 50 Word Story, Stand Up, Debate etc.

GENERAL RULES

  • All schools must be present for the opening and closing ceremony.
  • The competitions are open to students from grades 9 to 12.
  • The last date for submitting the entries will be specified for each event
  • Any kind of obscenity or vulgarity and (or) offensive language on or off the stage will disqualify the school.
  • The judges’ decision will be final.
  • Cre8 has two types of events available: Open events and school represented events
  • For the open event any number of people can participate from each school. If teams are involved the members should be from the same school, inter-school teams will not be accepted. Note that they will not be representing the school. The winners will receive individual prizes.
  • For open events:A Google form for each event has been provided.Please fill out the form and submit it
  • For school based events- you can register by giving your name directly to the principal or the person in charge

Future Project

The ability of the best innovators is to put their imagination on paper. To give their dreams and aspirations wings is what they are best known for. A man imagined to go beyond the stars, go beyond the stars man did.
Do you have it in you to explore the depressing darkness of Space, fight the elements and emerge safely out of your exploration? If so, you and your team are invited to create the vehicle that should take you to the deepest darkest corners of the world.
Battle it out with schools all over Kerala and let’s see which vehicle can fare the storm better!

Theme

  • Space Exploration

Guidelines

  • This is a team event, individual participation will not be allowed.
  • Only one team from each school is allowed to take part in the event.
  • Teams should have a number of members between 2 and 5.
  • Teams are requested to create an original model of their spacecraft.
  • The entries must be in the form of a prerecorded video submitted 17 days prior to the event date.
  • The entries must include
  • A concise explanation of the model made.
  • Materials used for making it.
  • How it would survive the extreme temperatures of space.
  • Technical functions it would perform.
  • And, the cost of making the model in real life.
  • The video must be under 5 minutes.

Grounds for Disqualification

  • Submitting the entry after the stipulated time
  • Failing to include the above-mentioned points in the entry
  • Exceeding the time limit

Judging criteria

  • Presentation -30 marks
  • Clarity in speaking
  • Knowledge depth
  • Explanation Practicality
  • Practicality -30 marks
  • The efficiency of the vehicle
  • Cost-Effectiveness
  • How conceivable the idea is.
  • Creativity -20 marks
  • Quality of model made -20 marks
  • Neatness
  • Shape and structure
  • Total -100 marks

Preliminary

  • Out of total teams, 15 teams will be shortlisted and selected for the final round of the event.
  • All entries will be put through a round of preliminary testing by a panel of the event heads and volunteers and will decide upon the final 15 that goes through to the final round.

Procedure

  • Once the entries are shortlisted and selected, it is passed to the judges for the final three winners.
  • The judges view the videos and the concerning team is immediately invited for a private meeting, where the judges may give their opinions and give negative as well as positive feedback.
  • The scores are lined up and put in an excel sheet.
  • In case of a tie the judge’s opinion wins and the winner is selected.

Quiz

A quiz is a form of game or mind sport in which players attempt to answer questions correctly about a certain or variety of subjects. Quizzes can be used as a brief assessment in education and similar fields to measure growth in knowledge, abilities, or skills.
Register Now

Rounds

General Round

  • n this round, each team has its own quota of 4 questions and other questions passed to it from the previous team that did not answer. A team gets 30 seconds to answer the question intended for it and is awarded 20 points for answering it. If the team, the question intended for gives a wrong answer, the quizmaster will give the correct answer. If the team that the question intended for passes it. The next team/s get 15 seconds to answer it and is awarded 10 points for the right answer. The team members can discuss before giving the answer. If a team cannot answer a question they can pass it or after 30 seconds it gets automatically passed to the next team. If a team is answering a question and the time passes, then the team gets to complete the answer and is awarded points for the right answer. There is no negative marking for the wrong answer.

Rapid Fire Round

  • Each team quota of 10 questions. In this round, each team will be asked 10 questions one after another. On the immediate completion of a question, each team gets 5 seconds to answer it. If a team is not able to answer a question, they can pass it for the next question. There is no team discussion in this round. 10 points is awarded for the correct answer. No negative points for the wrong answer. Once a question is passed the team cannot give an answer later.

Audio-Visual Round

  • 1 question each. Each Team will be shown a picture with a quotation. The team has to complete the quotation. The team has 15 seconds to complete the quotation. The teams will be made to listen to an audio clip. The team has to guess the name of the song or sing certain lines from the song as asked by the quiz master. Questions will not be passed to the next team. No negative points for wrong answers.
In case Of A Tie
  • In case of a tie, the teams that are tied will be asked to move onto another rapid-fire round.
    In this round, the rules remain the same as round 2 but each team only gets 5 questions and only 5 points are awarded for the correct answer.

Judging criteria

  • The marks for the candidates will be based on the following criteria:
  • should be an original story
  • eye-catching title is necessary
  • should be related to the theme of the event
  • colloquial language to be avoided (e.g.; ain’t, gonna)
